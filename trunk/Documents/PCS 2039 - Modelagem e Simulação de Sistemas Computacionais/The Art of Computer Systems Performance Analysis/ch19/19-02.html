19.1 PREPARING THE SIGN TABLE FOR A 2k–p DESIGN

The next step in understanding 2k–p designs is to learn to prepare tables with orthogonal columns. The procedure consists of the following two steps:

1.  Choose k – p factors and prepare a complete sign table for a full factorial design with k – p factors. This will result in a table of 2k–p rows and 2k–p columns. The first column will be marked I and consists of all I’s. The next k – p columns will be marked with the k – p factors that were chosen. The remaining columns are simply products of these factors.
2.  Of the 2k–p – k + p – 1 columns on the right, choose p columns and mark them with the p factors that were not chosen in step 1.
